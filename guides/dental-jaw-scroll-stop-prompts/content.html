Prompt A

Clean white background (#FFFFFF), soft studio lighting with subtle shadows beneath the jaw. The jaw model is anatomically accurate, pristine white porcelain-like material with a full set of 32 adult teeth — incisors, canines, premolars, and molars all perfectly aligned. The teeth have a natural enamel sheen with subtle translucency at the edges. Pink gum tissue (gingiva) is visible with realistic texture and color gradient from coral pink to pale pink near the tooth line. The jaw is closed in a natural occlusion position showing the bite alignment. Photorealistic rendering, 16:9 aspect ratio, medical-grade product catalog quality. Sharp focus across the entire model, subtle reflections on the glossy enamel surfaces. Clean, clinical, premium dental aesthetic — the kind of imagery used by high-end dental clinics. No text, no logos, no other objects in frame. Shot on Phase One IQ4 150MP, 120mm macro lens, f/8, studio strobe lighting with large softbox above and white bounce cards on sides. Ultra-sharp detail capturing individual tooth texture, enamel ridges, and gum contours. 8K quality downsampled to 4K.
Prompt B
Professional exploded-view product photography of a complete human dental jaw model, deconstructed into its individual anatomical components, all floating in space against a clean white background (#FFFFFF). Every component is visible and separated with even spacing: — Upper jaw bone (maxilla) — smooth ivory-white bone structure with empty tooth sockets visible — Lower jaw bone (mandible) — curved horseshoe shape with condyle processes and mental foramen visible — Individual teeth floating in anatomical groups: 4 incisors (flat, chisel-shaped), 2 canines (pointed), 4 premolars (two-cusped), 6 molars (multi-cusped with fissures) — for EACH jaw, totaling 32 teeth — Each tooth shows its full root structure below the crown — single roots for incisors, double/triple roots for molars — Pink gingival tissue segments (upper and lower arches) with realistic mucosal texture — Periodontal ligament fibers — thin translucent threads connecting tooth roots to bone — Alveolar bone segments showing the honeycomb-like internal spongy bone structure — Nerve bundles — the inferior alveolar nerve running through the mandibular canal, thin branching yellow fibers — Blood vessel network — fine red arterial branches feeding each tooth — Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) components — articular disc, condyle head, and joint capsule separated — Dental pulp chambers visible inside cross-sectioned teeth — pink-red tissue with nerve endings Each piece floats with even spacing between them, maintaining the general spatial relationship of where they sit in the assembled jaw. The arrangement follows a vertical explosion axis — bone at top and bottom, teeth in the middle layers, soft tissue and nerves between them. Soft studio lighting with subtle shadows on each floating piece. Components are pristine and detailed — you can see enamel ridges, root surface texture, bone porosity, and nerve fiber strands. The overall composition maintains the oval silhouette of the original jaw. Photorealistic rendering, 16:9 aspect ratio, medical illustration meets product photography. Shot on Phase One IQ4 150MP, focus-stacked for sharpness across all floating elements. Same lighting setup as the assembled shot for visual continuity.
Prompt C
START FRAME: A fully assembled anatomical dental jaw model (upper and lower jaw with all 32 teeth, pink gums, natural occlusion) sitting centered on a white background, premium product photography style, soft studio lighting. END FRAME: The same dental jaw elegantly deconstructed into an exploded view — every anatomical component floating in space: maxilla and mandible bones separated, all 32 individual teeth floating in their positional groups showing full root structures, gum tissue segments, nerve bundles, blood vessels, TMJ disc, and periodontal fibers — all separated along a vertical axis, maintaining spatial relationships. TRANSITION: Smooth, satisfying anatomical deconstruction animation. The jaw begins whole and still. After a brief pause (0.5s), the mandible slowly separates downward from the maxilla — the mouth elegantly opens. Then teeth begin to lift out of their sockets one by one in a cascading wave — starting from the front incisors and rippling back to the molars. As teeth float upward, they rotate slightly to reveal their root structures. The gum tissue peels away in smooth arches. Nerve fibers and blood vessels stretch and separate like delicate threads. The TMJ joint components drift apart last. Each piece lifts and floats outward along clean, deliberate paths. Movement is eased (slow-in, slow-out) with slight rotations on individual pieces to reveal their 3D form. The separation happens over 3 seconds in a cascading sequence, not all at once. Final floating arrangement holds for 1.5 seconds. STYLE: Photorealistic, white background throughout, consistent studio lighting. No camera movement — locked-off tripod shot. The only motion is the jaw deconstructing. Satisfying, ASMR-like precision. Clinical yet beautiful — premium dental clinic aesthetic meets medical visualization. DURATION: 5 seconds total. ASPECT RATIO: 16:9 QUALITY: High fidelity, smooth 24fps or higher, no artifacts. VARIATION — REVERSE (Assembly): Start with all components floating, then teeth slide into sockets with a satisfying click, gums wrap around, jaw closes into perfect occlusion. Equally compelling for dental clinic content. VARIATION — LOOP: Assemble → pause → deconstruct → pause → repeat seamlessly.
